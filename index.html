Step 2 of 3 — Record Your Loom

Read the questions below, then record one Loom answering all of them. Talk naturally — the more specific your stories and language, the better your posts will sound like you. Then paste the transcript below and generate.

Your Loom Prep Questions
📌
Pin 1 — Connect Post
Your identity, origin story + who you serve
1
What's your niche and modality? What do you actually do and who do you do it for? Be specific — name your approach, your framework, your lens.
2
What's your Human Design type and authority? And how does it show up in your business? (Optional but powerful.)
3
Tell your origin story. What were you struggling with before? What was the turning point that changed everything? What does your life and business look like now because of it?
4
Who is your ideal client? Describe them like you're talking about a real person. What keeps them stuck? What have they already tried that hasn't worked?
5
What content do you create and why should someone follow you? What will they get from being in your world? What topics do you consistently teach?
✨
Pin 2 — Compel Post
Client results + social proof
6
Share 3–5 client results or transformations. For each: their name or first name, what they were struggling with before, the specific result or shift after working with you, and a timeframe if you have one. Include any quotes they said if you remember them — those are gold.
7
What do your best clients all have in common? What was the thing that changed for them? What did they stop doing or start doing differently?
💫
Pin 3 — Convert Post
Your offers + how to work with you
8
Walk through every way someone can work with you or get value from you. Include your free community, every paid tier — name, what's inside, who it's for, price point. Talk through each one like you'd explain it to a friend.
9
What's your primary call to action? Your DM keyword and what they get, your free community link, your booking link — whatever you want people to do next.
10
What's the biggest objection someone might have before investing in working with you? How would you handle it?
